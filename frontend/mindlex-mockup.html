⚖️ Cyprus jurisdiction Plain tone

What are my main GDPR obligations as a SaaS founder selling to EU customers?
Short answer: You need a lawful basis to process data, a Privacy Policy, a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with sub-processors, and a way to handle data subject requests.

The four pillars are:
  1. Lawful basis — usually "contract" or "consent" depending on the feature.
  2. Transparency — clear, plain-language privacy notices at collection.
  3. Rights handling — access, deletion, portability within 30 days.
  4. Security — appropriate technical & organizational measures (encryption, access control, breach reporting).
Sources (Cyprus-prioritized): ↗ Cyprus DPA — Guidance for SMEs (2024) ↗ EU GDPR Art. 6, 12-22 • Internal: Mindlex Compliance Playbook
This is general information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified lawyer for your specific situation.

I just uploaded my draft Privacy Policy. Can you check it against GDPR?
I reviewed privacy-policy-v2.docx. Here's what I found:
⚖️ Compliance check (2 issues)
1. [Missing] Lawful basis disclosure
Source: §3 Data Collection — Your policy doesn't state the lawful basis for processing.
Suggested clause: "We process your personal data under Article 6(1)(b) GDPR — performance of the contract you entered into with us."
2. [Missing] Data retention period
Source: §5 Storage — No retention period mentioned.
Suggested clause: "We retain account data for 7 years after the contract ends, unless legal obligations require longer."
🛡️ Risk identification (1 high)
HIGH — Broad sub-processor transfer clause
Source: §7 Third Parties — Your clause allows transfer to "any service provider", which violates Art. 28(2).
Suggested rewrite: "We share data only with sub-processors listed at /sub-processors. We will provide 30 days' notice before adding any new sub-processor."
